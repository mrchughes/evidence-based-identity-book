Sequence Diagrams

Core Evidence Processing Workflows

The evidence-based identity platform orchestrates complex interactions between multiple services to transform citizen evidence into confident benefit decisions. The sequence diagrams illustrate how the platform's microservices coordinate to handle different scenarios while maintaining audit trails and managing uncertainty through confidence-based processing.

Each sequence represents a complete end-to-end workflow showing how evidence flows from initial submission through identity resolution, semantic translation, award calculation, and citizen notification. The diagrams demonstrate the platform's event-driven architecture where services communicate through well-defined APIs and message queues rather than tight coupling.

This sequence illustrates the platform's confidence-based processing where insufficient initial evidence triggers additional verification rather than automatic rejection. Cross-Product Evidence Reuse

The evidence reuse workflow shows the platform's core value proposition where citizens applying for additional benefits leverage previously verified evidence to streamline processing dramatically.

This workflow demonstrates how semantic translation enables evidence verified for BasicSupport to be appropriately interpreted for FamilyAssist assessment, reducing citizen burden while maintaining policy-specific calculation accuracy.

Retrospective Change of Circumstances

External evidence updates trigger automatic recalculation workflows that handle historical changes without requiring citizen action, demonstrating the platform's proactive approach to maintaining award accuracy.

This sequence shows how external evidence automatically triggers historical recalculation while routing significant discrepancies through human review to ensure appropriate handling of complex financial impacts.
