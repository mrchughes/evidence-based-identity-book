DCS Evidence-Based Identity Platform : Prototype Scope and Objectives

Created by Christian Hughes on Sept 15, 2025

Core Scope Definition

The prototype implementation focuses on a carefully selected subset of platform capabilities that demonstrate core value propositions while remaining manageable within a 6-month timeframe. The scope balances technical complexity with meaningful citizen impact to provide convincing evidence of the platform's potential.

Primary Scope Elements

Citizen Population:

  • 500 new BasicSupport applicants from the Manchester region

  • Mix of digital-native and digitally-assisted citizens to validate accessibility

  • Voluntary participation with clear opt-out procedures and alternative processing

Evidence Types:

  • Employment evidence (payslips, employer letters, P45 documents)

  • Identity verification (passport, driving license, birth certificates)

  • Address verification (council tax bills, utility bills, tenancy agreements)

  • Bank statements for income verification

Core Services:

  • Evidence Ingestion Service for document processing and assertion extraction

  • Identity Resolution Service for basic clustering and confidence scoring

  • Award Calculation Service for BasicSupport policy implementation

  • Investigation Queue Service for manual review workflows

Integration Points:

  • Citizen portal for evidence submission and application tracking

  • Caseworker interface for investigation and manual processing

  • Basic HMRC RTI integration for employment verification

  • Simplified notification service for status updates

Technical Architecture Subset

Infrastructure Components:

  • Single Kubernetes cluster with development and prototype environments

  • Apache Jena Fuseki triple store with basic high availability

  • PostgreSQL for operational data and award calculations

  • Redis for caching and session management

  • Basic monitoring and logging infrastructure

Security Implementation:

  • TLS encryption for all communications

  • Role-based access control for caseworkers and participants

  • Audit logging for all evidence processing and decisions

  • Data anonymization for reporting and analysis

Excluded from Prototype:

  • Cross-product evidence sharing (Child Benefit, AgeCare integration)

  • Advanced ML/AI capabilities and sophisticated fraud detection

  • Multi-region deployment and full disaster recovery

  • Complex partner integration beyond basic HMRC connection

Primary Objectives

Technical Validation Objectives

Architecture Proof-of-Concept: Validate that RDF triple store architecture can handle real citizen evidence with acceptable performance for basic processing workflows. Demonstrate that semantic web technologies provide practical benefits for government service delivery rather than theoretical advantages.

Confidence Scoring Effectiveness: Prove that confidence-based processing can accurately distinguish between high-quality and uncertain evidence, enabling appropriate automation while routing complex cases to human review. Validate confidence threshold settings for different evidence types and risk levels.

Evidence Reuse Capabilities: Demonstrate that citizens applying for additional services can benefit from previously verified evidence through intelligent pre-population and reduced documentation requirements. Show measurable time savings and improved citizen experience.

Operational Validation Objectives

Caseworker Workflow Integration: Validate that evidence-based processing enhances rather than complicates caseworker decision-making through comprehensive evidence presentation, confidence indicators, and investigation queue prioritization.

Citizen Experience Improvement: Prove that evidence-based processing delivers tangible benefits to citizens through faster processing, reduced documentation burden, and clearer communication about application status and requirements.

Training and Change Management: Develop and test training approaches for caseworkers while identifying change management strategies that support successful adoption of evidence-based working methods.

Business Value Demonstration

Processing Efficiency Gains: Measure concrete improvements in application processing time, caseworker productivity, and operational cost reduction that justify investment in full-scale implementation.

Decision Quality Enhancement: Demonstrate improved accuracy and consistency in benefit decisions through confidence-based processing and comprehensive evidence correlation.

Citizen Satisfaction Impact: Capture quantitative and qualitative evidence of improved citizen experience through reduced burden, faster service, and enhanced transparency about decision-making processes.
