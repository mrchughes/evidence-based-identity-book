DCS Evidence-Based Identity Platform : Building Evidence-Based Services for a Unified Citizen Experience

Created by Christian Hughes, last modified on Sept 17, 2025

Overview

The imaginary Department of Citizen Services should transform from fragmented evidence silos to a unified evidence-based inference engine that would maintain the illusion of definitive decisions while managing probabilistic reality underneath. This platform proposal represents a fundamental shift from record-based systems to evidence-based reasoning, enabling citizens and caseworkers to work with familiar binary outcomes while the system becomes intelligent about uncertainty and risk.

At its core, this transformation would address a critical challenge: DCS's current federation shares policy-interpreted attributes rather than raw evidence, creating fragmentation and preventing legitimate policy divergence. Citizens repeatedly provide identical evidence across different DCS services, while caseworkers manually process retrospective changes and investigate conflicts that could be automatically detected and prioritized.

The proposed evidence-based identity platform would treat all information as simple statements of "X said Y about Z" with full context about who said it, when, and how they verified it. Raw evidence would accumulate once across all DCS services, different policies could interpret the same evidence appropriately for their specific needs, and benefit awards would only happen when the system has high confidence about both who someone is and their circumstances.

Key Principles

Managed Complexity: Citizens and caseworkers would experience simple, definitive interfaces while sophisticated probabilistic reasoning operates behind the scenes to manage uncertainty and build confidence over time.

Evidence Before Attributes: The proposed system would share raw verified facts with full provenance rather than policy-specific interpretations, enabling legitimate policy divergence while preventing evidence fragmentation.

Confidence-Based Decisions: High confidence thresholds would protect both citizens and DCS by ensuring benefit payments only occur when evidence quality justifies the financial risk involved.

Semantic Flexibility: Different DCS services could interpret the same evidence according to their specific policy needs through automated semantic translation, preserving policy autonomy while enabling evidence reuse.

Explicit Uncertainty: Rather than hiding uncertainty behind false certainty, the proposed system would acknowledge and manage probabilistic reality through systematic confidence scoring and exception-based investigation workflows.

Transformation Impact

This platform would enable DCS to provide faster, more consistent service to citizens while reducing operational costs and improving fraud detection. Citizens would experience dramatic reductions in re-asking for evidence they've already provided, while caseworkers would gain intelligent tools that focus their attention where human judgment adds the most value.

The approach would prove that government services can work with reality rather than against it, creating the foundation for citizen-centric services that scale across departments while preserving the accountability and transparency that democratic governance requires.
