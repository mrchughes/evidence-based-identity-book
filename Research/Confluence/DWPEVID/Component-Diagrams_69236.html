Component Diagrams

Evidence Ingestion Service Architecture

The Evidence Ingestion Service transforms diverse evidence inputs into standardized RDF assertions while maintaining complete provenance and applying appropriate confidence scoring based on source credibility and verification methods.

The Evidence Ingestion Service handles the complex transformation of unstructured evidence into semantic assertions while preserving complete audit trails and applying sophisticated confidence scoring that accounts for source credibility, verification methods, and document quality indicators.

Identity Resolution Service Architecture

The Identity Resolution Service implements probabilistic clustering algorithms that correlate evidence across different subject identifiers while managing uncertainty through confidence scoring and supporting both automatic correlation and manual review workflows.

The Identity Resolution Service balances automated clustering efficiency with accuracy through machine learning models, graph analysis, and sophisticated confidence assessment that accounts for evidence quality, temporal factors, and correlation strength.

Award Calculation Service Architecture

The Award Calculation Service implements complex policy engines that determine benefit entitlements based on confidence thresholds while supporting simulation capabilities and comprehensive audit requirements for financial decisions.

The Award Calculation Service integrates sophisticated policy engines with confidence-based processing to ensure that financial decisions occur only when evidence quality meets appropriate thresholds while maintaining complete audit trails for accountability and appeals.

Investigation Queue Service Architecture

The Investigation Queue Service manages complex exception workflows through intelligent case routing, workflow orchestration, and outcome tracking that ensures human attention focuses on cases requiring judgment and expertise.

The Investigation Queue Service implements sophisticated workflow management that balances automated efficiency with human expertise through intelligent case routing, priority management, and comprehensive outcome tracking.

Semantic Translation Service Architecture

The Semantic Translation Service enables evidence sharing between different policy contexts through formal ontology management, reasoning engines, and translation validation that preserves semantic meaning while accommodating vocabulary differences.

The Semantic Translation Service enables sophisticated cross-context evidence sharing through formal ontology management and reasoning capabilities while maintaining semantic precision and comprehensive audit trails for all translation decisions.

These component diagrams illustrate the internal architecture of each core service, showing how sophisticated capabilities like probabilistic identity resolution, confidence-based award calculation, and semantic translation are implemented through well-designed component interactions that maintain modularity, scalability, and audit requirements essential for government operations.
