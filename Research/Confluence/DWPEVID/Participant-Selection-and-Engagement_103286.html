DCS Evidence-Based Identity Platform : Participant Selection and Engagement

Created by Christian Hughes on Sept 15, 2025

Citizen Participant Criteria

The prototype requires careful participant selection to ensure representative results while managing complexity and providing meaningful evidence of platform capabilities. Participant criteria balance diversity with operational manageability to deliver credible outcomes.

Primary Selection Criteria

Geographic Focus: Manchester region selected for concentrated delivery and simplified logistics with established DCS office presence for face-to-face support when needed. Geographic concentration enables effective prototype management while providing sufficient population diversity.

Application Type: New BasicSupport applicants who haven't previously interacted with DCS services, enabling clear measurement of evidence-based processing benefits without complications from existing case history or established workflows.

Digital Capability: Mix of digital-native participants (60%) and digitally-assisted participants (40%) to validate that evidence-based improvements reach all citizen populations rather than only those comfortable with technology.

Demographic Representation:

  • Age range: 18-65 with proportional representation across age bands

  • Employment status: Mix of unemployed, part-time, and variable income participants

  • Family composition: Single adults, couples, and families with children

  • Language and accessibility needs: Include participants requiring interpretation or accessibility support

Engagement and Consent Process

Voluntary Participation:

  • Clear explanation of prototype purpose and citizen benefits

  • Explicit consent for evidence-based processing with opt-out procedures

  • Alternative processing through existing systems for non-participants

  • No disadvantage for choosing not to participate in prototype

Informed Consent Management:

  • Plain English explanation of evidence sharing and automated processing

  • Clear description of confidence-based decision making and human oversight

  • Participant rights including access to evidence, correction procedures, and appeals

  • Data protection information and retention policies

Ongoing Engagement:

  • Regular check-ins about participant experience and satisfaction

  • Feedback collection through surveys, interviews, and focus groups

  • Citizen advisory group with representative participants providing design input

  • Transparent communication about prototype results and future plans

Caseworker Participation

Selection and Training

Caseworker Selection:

  • Volunteer caseworkers from Manchester office with basic digital skills

  • Mix of experience levels from new staff to senior caseworkers

  • Representatives from different specializations including identity verification and complex case resolution

  • Champions who can provide feedback and support broader adoption

Comprehensive Training Program:

  • Evidence-based processing concepts and confidence scoring interpretation

  • New investigation interface and evidence presentation tools

  • Updated decision-making procedures incorporating confidence thresholds

  • Change management support and continuous learning opportunities

Performance Support:

  • Ongoing coaching and feedback during prototype operation

  • Regular workshops sharing lessons learned and best practices

  • Access to technical support and system expertise

  • Career development opportunities related to evidence-based processing

Change Management Strategy

Cultural Adaptation:

  • Clear communication about how evidence-based processing enhances rather than replaces caseworker expertise

  • Demonstration of improved decision support through comprehensive evidence presentation

  • Recognition and reward for successful adoption and innovation

  • Peer learning networks supporting knowledge sharing

Workflow Integration:

  • Gradual introduction of new tools and procedures with parallel operation initially

  • Flexibility to use existing processes when new tools are insufficient

  • Regular workflow assessment and optimization based on caseworker feedback

  • Integration with existing performance management and quality assurance processes
