DCS Evidence-Based Identity Platform : Business Architecture

Created by Christian Hughes, last modified on Sept 17, 2025

The Business Architecture outlines a transformative operating model that fundamentally reimagines how DCS delivers services to citizens. Moving from product-centric silos to evidence-centric operations, we create a framework where different benefit areas collaborate around shared citizen evidence while maintaining their distinct policy responsibilities. This enables us to preserve departmental autonomy while providing the evidence sharing and coordination that citizens rightfully expect from government services.

At the heart of this transformation is a comprehensive capability model structured around three interconnected pillars. First, our core business capabilities of Evidence Accumulation, Intelligent Identity Resolution, and Semantic Translation form the foundation that enables all other improvements. Second, our operational capabilities implement Exception-Based Case Management, Cross-System Pattern Recognition, and Automated Retrospective Processing that fundamentally change how work gets done. Third, our strategic capabilities including Adaptive Confidence Management and Policy-Agnostic Evidence Processing create organizational agility that enables rapid adaptation to changing requirements.

This business architecture introduces a confidence-based decision authority framework where explicit thresholds determine when automated processing can proceed versus when human intervention is required. Different benefit types and award amounts have different confidence requirements, with higher-value awards requiring stronger evidence verification. This approach focuses precious human attention where it adds the most value - on complex cases requiring judgment - while enabling efficient automation of routine processing. The result is a more responsive organization that delivers better citizen outcomes while improving operational efficiency and fraud prevention.
