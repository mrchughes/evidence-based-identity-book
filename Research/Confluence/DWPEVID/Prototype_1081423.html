DCS Evidence-Based Identity Platform : Prototype

Prototype Implementation Proposal

Executive Summary

The evidence-based identity platform represents a fundamental transformation in how DCS would process citizen evidence and manage benefit decisions. While the comprehensive architecture demonstrates the platform's potential, the complexity and scale of change require careful validation through a focused prototype implementation before committing to full-scale deployment.

This prototype proposal outlines a 6-month proof-of-concept implementation that would validate core platform assumptions while delivering immediate value to a limited citizen population. The prototype focuses on BasicSupport (Universal Credit equivalent) evidence processing for new applicants, demonstrating evidence reuse, confidence-based processing, and citizen experience improvements within a controlled environment.

Prototype Objectives:

  • Validate technical architecture with real citizen evidence and processing workflows

  • Demonstrate confidence-based processing effectiveness and accuracy

  • Prove evidence reuse capabilities and citizen experience improvements

  • Establish operational procedures and staff training approaches

  • Identify optimization opportunities and implementation lessons for full deployment

Expected Outcomes:

  • 500 citizen participants experiencing evidence-based application processing

  • 30% reduction in evidence re-submission for returning participants

  • 50% processing time improvement for high-confidence applications

  • Validated confidence scoring algorithms with 85% accuracy

  • Comprehensive lessons learned for full-scale implementation

The prototype provides concrete evidence of platform benefits while managing implementation risk through limited scope and comprehensive monitoring. Success metrics and lessons learned would inform the full implementation strategy while demonstrating value to stakeholders and building confidence in the evidence-based approach.
