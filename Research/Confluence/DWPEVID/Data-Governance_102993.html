DCS Evidence-Based Identity Platform : Data Governance

Created by Christian Hughes, last modified on Sept 15, 2025

Governance Framework and Accountability

The evidence-based identity platform requires comprehensive data governance that balances the need for evidence sharing and automated decision-making with strict protection of citizen privacy, data accuracy, and regulatory compliance. The governance framework establishes clear accountability structures, decision-making processes, and quality standards that ensure the platform operates within legal and ethical boundaries while delivering effective public services.

Data governance operates through a hierarchical structure with the Data Protection Officer maintaining overall responsibility for privacy compliance, business data owners accountable for policy interpretation and decision quality, technical data stewards responsible for system implementation and data quality, and operational teams managing day-to-day evidence processing and citizen interaction.

The governance framework distinguishes between different types of data requiring different protection levels including raw evidence requiring maximum protection and retention, identity correlation data requiring careful anonymization and access control, derived insights needing statistical disclosure control, and system metadata requiring security but not privacy protection.

Governance Structure:

  • Data Protection Board: Senior leadership oversight for privacy and compliance decisions

  • Evidence Quality Committee: Cross-product coordination for evidence standards and confidence thresholds

  • Technical Architecture Review: Approval authority for changes affecting data processing or storage

  • Citizen Rights Panel: Independent review of algorithmic decisions and privacy protection effectiveness

Decision-making processes ensure that changes to confidence thresholds, semantic mappings, or evidence requirements undergo appropriate review with impact assessment for citizen outcomes, privacy implications, legal compliance, and operational effectiveness. Automated governance workflows track decision approval status and implementation progress while maintaining comprehensive audit trails.

Privacy Protection and Data Protection Compliance

The platform implements privacy by design principles with multiple layers of protection that operate even if individual components are compromised. Privacy protection begins with data minimization during evidence collection, continues through purpose limitation during processing, and extends to secure deletion when retention periods expire.

Privacy by Design Implementation:

  • Decentralized Identity: Citizens control their digital identity without requiring central government identity infrastructure

  • Granular Consent: Citizens authorize specific evidence sharing for particular purposes with clear expiration dates

  • Selective Disclosure: Evidence sharing includes only information necessary for specific decisions

  • Automated Deletion: Evidence automatically expires and is securely deleted according to retention schedules

Data protection compliance operates through comprehensive data protection impact assessments, regular compliance audits, citizen rights management, and breach detection and response procedures. The platform's RDF-based architecture enables precise data lineage tracking that supports subject access requests and data portability requirements.

Citizen rights management enables individuals to exercise their data protection rights through automated processing where possible and manual review for complex cases. Citizens can access complete records of their evidence, request corrections to inaccurate information, withdraw consent for specific data sharing, and receive portable copies of their data in machine-readable formats.

Data Quality Management

Data quality governance ensures that evidence maintains appropriate accuracy, completeness, and reliability standards throughout its lifecycle. Quality management operates through automated validation, statistical monitoring, source credibility assessment, and continuous improvement processes that enhance decision-making effectiveness while reducing errors.

Quality Dimensions and Metrics:

  • Accuracy: Percentage of evidence assertions that remain unchallenged after citizen review and appeal processes

  • Completeness: Proportion of identity clusters meeting confidence thresholds for automated processing

  • Consistency: Correlation between similar evidence from different sources measured through cross-validation

  • Timeliness: Age distribution of evidence supporting active award calculations

Quality monitoring tracks evidence quality patterns across different sources, identifies systematic issues requiring partner attention, and measures the effectiveness of quality improvement initiatives. Statistical process control identifies unusual quality patterns that might indicate data processing problems or changes in external source reliability.

Access Control and Authorization

Access control governance ensures that citizen evidence is accessed only by authorized individuals for legitimate purposes while maintaining comprehensive audit trails that support accountability and investigation capabilities. The authorization framework operates through role-based access control, attribute-based filtering, and dynamic authorization that adapts to case assignments and citizen consent.

Authorization Levels:

  • Full Access: Assigned caseworkers can view all evidence for cases in their workload

  • Limited Access: Specialist teams access specific evidence types relevant to their expertise

  • Aggregated Access: Management and policy teams access statistical summaries without individual-level data

  • Audit Access: Independent auditors and appeals officers access complete records for accountability reviews

Dynamic authorization adjusts access permissions based on case assignments, investigation status, and citizen consent preferences. When citizens authorize evidence sharing with external partners, temporary access permissions are created with automatic expiration and comprehensive logging of all access activities.

Audit trail requirements ensure that all data access is logged with sufficient detail to support investigation of potential privacy violations, unauthorized access, or data misuse. Access logs include user identification, accessed data scope, business justification, timestamp information, and outcome recording.

Retention and Disposal Policies

Data retention governance balances legal requirements, operational needs, and privacy protection through sophisticated lifecycle management that accounts for different evidence types, retention obligations, and deletion requirements. Retention policies implement both regulatory compliance and operational efficiency while supporting citizen rights to data erasure.

Retention Categories:

  • Evidence Assertions: 7-year retention for benefit calculation audit requirements with archive storage after 2 years

  • Identity Clusters: Indefinite retention with periodic confidence recalculation and quality review

  • Award Decisions: 7-year retention with complete audit trail preservation for appeals and compliance

  • Investigation Records: 10-year retention with data minimization after case closure

  • Consent Records: Lifetime retention to demonstrate lawful basis for historical processing

  • System Audit Logs: 7-year retention with compression and summarization after 1 year

Automated disposal processes implement secure deletion when retention periods expire, with verification procedures that ensure complete removal from all system components including backups and cache systems. Disposal certificates provide audit evidence that retention obligations have been met while citizen privacy has been protected.

Exception handling manages cases where standard retention periods may be extended due to ongoing investigations, appeals, or legal proceedings. Extension approvals require appropriate authorization with documented justification and automatic review procedures to prevent indefinite retention without legal basis.

Compliance Monitoring and Reporting

Governance compliance operates through continuous monitoring, automated reporting, and regular audit procedures that ensure the platform meets all regulatory requirements while identifying opportunities for improvement. Compliance monitoring covers privacy protection, data quality, access control effectiveness, and retention policy implementation.

Automated Compliance Reporting:

  • Weekly Privacy Reports: Data processing activities, consent status, and subject rights requests

  • Monthly Quality Reports: Evidence accuracy metrics, source reliability trends, and quality incidents

  • Quarterly Access Reports: Authorization patterns, unusual access activities, and audit findings

  • Annual Compliance Review: Comprehensive assessment of governance effectiveness and regulatory compliance

Statistical disclosure control ensures that compliance reporting and analytical insights protect individual privacy while providing necessary transparency for accountability and improvement. Differential privacy techniques enable population-level analysis without compromising individual citizen information.
